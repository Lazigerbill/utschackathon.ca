Rules

Team Formation

Students will sign up individually. At the hackathon you will form teams composed of at least 2 CMS and 2 Management students, up to a maximum of 7 students total. You will have the opportunity at the beginning of the hackathon to pitch your ideas and listen to others' ideas for digital financial literacy product to help you select teams.

Prior to #UTSCFinanceHacks

To ensure fairness please do not begin work on code, design elements or business and marketing plans before the event. However, you are encouraged to research and brainstorm to come up with a pitch for a digital financial literacy product.

Data sources and other external resources

You are encouraged to use third party services, frameworks, APIs and open sources of data. Please see the list of data sources and resource that will be available on our website during the event. Special thanks to our partners who provided access to financial data.

Code of Conduct

Demonstrate professionalism and be respectful of your fellow students and their ideas. Provide encouragement and constructive criticism for the benefit of the other attendees. University of Toronto is an inclusive environment and harassment in any form will not be tolerated.

Challenges

The following are some of the most common financial challenges Canadians face; solve them and you'll help Canadians learn how to manage their personal finances successfully. That said, these are suggestions, not restrictions: fit the theme, come up with a working solution - and you'll get to demo.

  • manage money and debt wisely
  • plan and save for the future
  • prevent and protect against fraud and financial abuse
  • accessibility to securities market information
  • make better investment decisions
